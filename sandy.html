NORTHWESTERN COLLEGE THEATRE DRAMATIZES OLD TESTAMENT STORIES    
    
The Drama Ministries Ensemble (DME) of Northwestern College, Orange City, Iowa, will
perform stories from the Old Testament at Family of God Community Church, 90 Quarterline Rd. in
Newaygo, on Sunday, March 1, at 6:30 p.m. A “Sloppy Joe” Supper will precede the event; serving
begins at 5:30. Community members are invited to both the supper and free performance.

In addition to staging the tragedy of the first brothers, Cain and Abel, the DME actors will also
perform more of the Old Testament’s most dramatic accounts such as the binding of Isaac by his father
Abraham, the killing of Goliath, and Daniel’s survival of the fiery furnace. Each drama is scripted directly
from Scripture.

The scripting and performance of the dramas is part of a larger worship drama effort called the
Ancient Hebrew Drama Project, led by Northwestern theatre professor and DME director Jeff Barker and
Dr. Tom Boogaart, professor of Old Testament studies at Western Theological Seminary in Holland,
Mich. Barker and Boogaart are co-authoring a book tentatively titled “Sarah Laughs Again: The Rebirth
of Ancient Biblical Drama.” The Old Testament story of Sarah’s skeptical, humorous response to
becoming pregnant will also be performed by the Northwestern theatre company.

Northwestern’s DME theatre company has performed across the U.S. and in Japan and Ethiopia.
Rated a top-10 Midwestern college by U.S. News & World Report magazine, Northwestern provides an
education committed to academic rigor and a Christian worldview. The theatre department offers
programs in theatre, theatre and speech, Christian theatre ministries, and arts administration.
